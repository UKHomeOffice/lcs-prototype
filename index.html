End a tenancy due to immigration status

Use this service to end a tenancy if you think that someone living in a property that you own or manage is not allowed to stay in England due to their immigration status (‘disqualified person’). A tenancy can be a verbal or written agreement. This service is only for rental property in England.

Warning You could face an unlimited fine or up to five years in prison if you knowingly allow someone who is not allowed to stay in England to live in your property.

This service will allow you to:

    report that a disqualified person has left your property

    Tell the Home Office that a person has left your property following a Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person. This means that Home Office can update their records and you have no further actions.

    take action if you think a person may be disqualified from renting

    If you believe that a person may be disqualified from renting due to their immigration status you can request a Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person from the Home Office to confirm this. You can then use this to help you end the tenancy.

    check if a person living in your property is still disqualified from renting

    If you have received a Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person to confirm that someone living in your property is not allowed to rent due to their immigration status, you can check that this is still valid. You may wish to do this if some time has passed since it was issued and you are using it to end a tenancy.

    tell the Home Office that a tenant has failed the follow-up right to rent check

    If a tenant has failed the follow-up right to rent check you need to make a report to the Home Office. The Home Office will send you a Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person to help you end the tenancy.

Who can use this service

  • landlords
  • agents acting on behalf of the landlords

Before you start

You will need the following:

  • name of your tenant or occupier
  • property address
  • tenancy start date
  • landlord or agent details
  • Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person (if you have one)

The following is also useful if you have it:

  • date of birth and nationality of your tenant or occupier
  • Home Office reference number for your tenant or occupier
    • What's a Home Office reference number?

    If the person has applied for a visa with the Home Office, they will be issued with a reference number.
