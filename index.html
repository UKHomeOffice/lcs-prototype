End a tenancy agreement due to immigration status

Use this service to end a tenancy agreement if you think that someone living in a property that you own or manage is not allowed to stay in England due to their immigration status. This service is only for rental property in England.

Warning You could face an unlimited fine or up to five years in prison if you knowingly allow someone who is not allowed to stay in England to live in your property.

This service will allow you to:

  • report that tenants or occupiers have left your property following a Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person
  • check if a Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person is still valid
  • request a Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person so that you can give tenants or occupiers notice to leave your property due to their immigration status

Who can use this service

  • landlords
  • agents acting on behalf of the landlords

Before you start

You will need the following:

  • details of your tenant or occupier including name, date of birth and nationality (if known) and their Home Office reference number (if applicable and known)
  • details of other people under the same tenancy agreement
  • property address
  • tenancy start date
  • landlord or agent details
  • Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person (if you have one)
